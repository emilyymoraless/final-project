Why Bitwise?

Initially I had planned on becoming a teacher, specifically for elementary children since I had done a lot of volunteer work at elementary schools during high school. I attended Sacramento State after I graduated high school in 2016 with the intention of obtaining a bachelor's degree in Liberal Studies. I finished about 3 semesters before I decided that it was not for me after all. The classes were not exciting to me and I didn't feel like I was being challenged. During my fourth semester I was convinced computer science was the career for me, it was a challenging career and it required lots of math units which I loved. During that time, I also found out I was pregnant so my college plan was put on hold. I moved back to my hometown and started looking for jobs instead. After my son was born I worked at a jewelry store for about a year, then a bridal boutique. After a while I ended up being a stay-at-home mom instead. While at home I had a lot of time to think about what my next move was. I knew I wanted to become a software engineer and thought about going back to school; but life happened and I now needed to find a way to work, in order to provide for my son and I, while also trying to pick up coding skills. While at work and discussing my interest in coding with my boss, she mentioned she had received an email about Bitwise offering courses. She then forwarded that email to me and I looked over the Bitwise site and instantly knew it was perfect for me; classes twice a week, class from 6pm to 9pm, the very inviting community.
